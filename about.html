Overview of Tudun Wada LGA

Our History

Tudun Wada Local Government Area was established in 1996 following the local government reforms in Nigeria. Named after the historic Tudun Wada settlement, our LGA has evolved from a small trading post to a vibrant administrative center in Kano State. For over two decades, we have served as the governmental authority responsible for the administration and development of the region.

Location & Demographics

Strategically located in Kano State, Tudun Wada LGA covers approximately 1,200 square kilometers. We are bordered by Doguwa, Kiru, and Karaye Local Government Areas. Our population of approximately 280,000 residents (based on the latest census data) is primarily engaged in agriculture, trade, and small-scale industries. The LGA is home to diverse ethnic groups with Hausa-Fulani being the predominant cultural influence.

Our Vision

To transform Tudun Wada into a model local government characterized by sustainable development, good governance, and improved quality of life for all residents.

Our Mission

We are committed to providing efficient and transparent governance while facilitating community-driven development initiatives that promote economic growth, education, healthcare, and infrastructure development in Tudun Wada LGA.

Core Values

Integrity: We uphold the highest standards of honesty, transparency, and ethical conduct in all our operations.

Service: We are dedicated to serving our community with compassion, respect, and professionalism.

Innovation: We embrace creative solutions and modern approaches to address complex challenges facing our community.

Inclusivity: We ensure that all residents, regardless of background or status, have equal access to opportunities and services.

Accountability: We take responsibility for our actions and are answerable to the people we serve.

Governance Structure

The governance structure of Tudun Wada Local Government Area consists of two main arms:

1. Executive Arm

Led by the Executive Chairman, the Executive Arm is responsible for policy implementation, day-to-day administration, and service delivery. It includes the Vice Chairman, Secretary to the Local Government, Heads of Departments, and other appointed officials.

1. Executive Arm

Led by the Executive Chairman, the Executive Arm is responsible for policy implementation, day-to-day administration, and service delivery. It includes the Vice Chairman, Secretary to the Local Government, Heads of Departments, and other appointed officials.

2. Legislative Arm

The Legislative Arm consists of elected Councillors representing different wards within the Local Government Area. It is responsible for making bylaws, approving the budget, and providing oversight functions. The Legislative Council is headed by the Leader of the Council.

This dual structure ensures checks and balances in governance and promotes democratic principles in local administration.
